About Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that operates on a technology called blockchain. It was introduced in 2009 by an anonymous person or group of people using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto.

Bitcoin offers the promise of a secure, transparent, and borderless financial system. It enables peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries such as banks, making it a disruptive force in the world of finance.

Bitcoin's history dates back to a whitepaper published by Satoshi Nakamoto in October 2008, titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System." This whitepaper outlined the vision for a digital currency that would operate without a central authority.

The underlying technology behind Bitcoin is blockchain, which is a decentralized ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers. This technology ensures the security and integrity of the Bitcoin network.
